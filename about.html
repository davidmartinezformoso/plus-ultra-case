What Is an Evidence Board?

An evidence board is a visual tool used to map relationships, evidence, and key figures in an investigation. Each card represents a person, location, document, or asset. Red strings connect cards where a direct relationship has been documented in public sources. The board is designed to help readers understand complex, multi-party cases at a glance.

How to Read a Board

Each card contains the following fields in order: a portrait photo or representative image; the subject's name in uppercase; their role during the alleged period of conduct; three headline statistics; a "What We Know" section with confirmed sourced facts; an "Under Investigation" section with contested or open questions; and numbered source links.

Strings connect cards where a documented relationship exists. White glowing strings indicate primary connections; grey strings indicate secondary or supporting relationships. String labels name the nature of the relationship in two or three words.

A Note on Positions Shown

The role shown on each card is the position held during the alleged period of conduct, not the person's current role. A person may have left office, been removed from their position, or had their title changed since the events depicted. This is intentional: it reflects their authority and context at the time the relevant conduct is alleged to have occurred.

Methodology

All information on these boards is drawn from public sources: court filings, government press releases, legislative records, established news reporting, and Wikipedia articles with cited primary sources. Every factual claim is attributed to a numbered source linked directly on each card and listed in the references section at the bottom of the board.

No anonymous sources are used. Statistics, charges, and convictions are not invented or extrapolated — only facts that appear in the cited public record are presented as fact. Where information is contested, alleged, or unresolved, it is placed in the "Under Investigation" section with appropriate hedging language.

Legal Notice

All persons shown on these boards are presumed innocent unless a court of competent jurisdiction has entered a final finding of guilt. Inclusion on this board does not imply criminal culpability. Civil settlements do not constitute admissions of wrongdoing unless explicitly stated in court documents. This site does not constitute legal advice.
