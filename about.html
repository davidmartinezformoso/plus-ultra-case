What Is an Evidence Board?

An evidence board — also called a “link chart,” “conspiracy board,” or “murder board” — is a visual investigative tool used by law enforcement agencies, intelligence analysts, and investigative journalists to map the relationships between individuals, organisations, events, and pieces of evidence in a complex case.

The purpose is to make visible the connections that are difficult to hold in mind simultaneously: who knew whom, who paid whom, which organisations overlapped, and what physical evidence links which actors to which events. By laying this out spatially, investigators can spot patterns, identify missing links, and communicate findings clearly to others.

Evidence boards have been used in major organised crime investigations, corruption inquiries, financial fraud cases, and terrorism proceedings worldwide. The format used on this site — cards connected by lines on a dark background — is the classic representation familiar from real investigative practice.

How to Read a Board

Each board on this site is built from two types of card: suspect cards (white background) and evidence cards (light grey background). Lines connecting cards represent documented relationships or interactions as reported in the cited sources.

Photo Official or press photo of the individual, or a representative image for evidence items. All photos are greyscale.
Name Full name of the individual or label for the evidence item.
Role / Title The position held during the period of alleged conduct. See note below.
Allegations The most significant alleged conduct, condensed to 2–3 bullet points from cited sources.
Stats Three key figures that give rapid context: amounts, dates, or status indicators.
What We Know Confirmed facts reported by multiple sources or stated in court documents.
Under Investigation Open questions that judicial or prosecutorial authorities are actively pursuing.
Sources [n] Numbered footnotes linking directly to the underlying news article or court document.

Connecting lines between cards are labelled where the nature of the relationship is clear (e.g. “PRESSURE,” “OWNER,” “KEY ALLIANCE”). A line does not imply criminal culpability — it indicates a documented connection relevant to the investigation.

A Note on Positions Shown

Important The title or position displayed beneath each individual’s name reflects the role that person held during the period when the alleged conduct is reported to have taken place — not necessarily their current position at the time of viewing.

This is standard practice in investigative journalism and law enforcement documentation. Showing the position held at the time of the alleged conduct is essential for understanding the significance of the alleged actions: whether an individual had authority over a decision, access to certain resources, or a specific duty of care.

For example, a person described as “Minister of Transport” reflects that the alleged conduct involved decisions made while they held that ministerial post — even if they no longer hold that position today. Similarly, a person described as “Former President” reflects that their alleged conduct relates to activities carried out during or shortly after their time in office.

This approach does not imply that the individual currently holds the position shown, nor that their actions in any subsequent role are relevant to the case.

Understanding Status Labels

The header of each board shows the overall case status. Individual cards also carry status stamps. These labels have the following meanings:

Active Case
Formal criminal proceedings have been opened by a competent judicial authority and are ongoing. One or more individuals have been formally cited as investigated persons or defendants.
Closed Case
Judicial proceedings have concluded, either with a verdict, an acquittal, or a decision to discontinue proceedings.
Arrested / Detained
The individual has been taken into custody by law enforcement authorities in connection with the investigation.
Indicted
A formal indictment has been issued by a competent authority charging the individual with specific criminal offences.
Sanctioned
The individual has been placed on a sanctions list by a governmental authority, restricting financial transactions, asset holdings, or travel.
Under Scrutiny
The individual has not been formally charged but is subject to active judicial or prosecutorial inquiry as part of the broader investigation.
Wanted
A warrant has been issued for the individual’s arrest by a competent authority, and the individual has not yet been apprehended.

Our Methodology

Boards on this site are assembled exclusively from publicly available information: court documents, official judicial orders, government press releases, established news reporting, and publicly available law enforcement indictments.

Every claim displayed on a card is referenced by a numbered footnote linking to the underlying source. No anonymous sources, speculative claims, or unverified allegations are included. Where the status of an allegation is uncertain, the language used reflects that uncertainty (“allegedly,” “reportedly,” “under investigation”).

Sources are assessed for credibility before inclusion. Preference is given to primary sources (court filings, official statements) over secondary reporting. Where only secondary sources are available, multiple independent sources are required for a claim to be included.

Boards are updated as cases develop. The date of last update is shown in the board header. Claims that were accurate at publication may become outdated as judicial proceedings evolve.
